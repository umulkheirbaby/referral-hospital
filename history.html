Our History

Garissa Level 5 Teaching and Referral Hospital formerly known as Garissa Provincial General Hospital. The hospital has a catchment population of 52,919 with an estimated average new outpatient workload of 10,588 and inpatient of 1,073 Monthly respectively. It has bed capacity of 300 with 82% bed occupancy.

The County Referral hospital was constructed in 1969 by the government of Kenya under Nairobi City council and it was taken over by ministry of health in 1970 and then devolved in 2013 immediately after devolution. The County Referral hospital is categorized as level 5 in the ministry of health norms and standard for health services delivery. This level serves a broader spectrum of specialized referral and curative services. The hospital is a training institution for Intern medical doctors, clinical officers and nurses/plus other cadres. The expected population served by this facility is 1,000,000 persons plus the specialized services on the bases of defined need and not just the expected demand.

Apart from North Eastern Region, The hospital also serves the neighboring Counties of Kitui, Tana River Isiolo, Wajir and Mandera.The hospital is also a Referral Centre for Refugee population of Dadaab estimated to be over 355,052 persons. The Hospital is attached to 3 Community unit( CU)

  1. The Hospital has a Regional Cancer center which serves a wider population across the region
  2. Modern Eye unit has been constructed though needs.
  3. Mortuary was also constructed to provide embalmment, postmortems and preservation of bodies with a capacity of 12 bodies.
The hospital offers a wide array of services.These include
  • Outpatient services
  • In patient services
  • Health Records and Information services
  • Rehabilitative services
  • Diagnostic services
  • Psychiatry
  • Surgery
  • Obstetric and gynecological
  • Specialized services (CT scan center and MRI, Renal services, Oncology, ICU).
  • The facility is also a satellite for blood transfusion for the region.
The hospital just like any other institutions faces a number of challenges including
  1. Lack of amenity ward for the staffs
  2. Inadequate staffing
  3. Inadequate ambulances and utility vehicles
  4. Poor social economic status of its client who are not in a position to meet medical bills
  5. Inadequate medical equipment among others.
